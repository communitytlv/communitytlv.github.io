SPOTLIGHT TALK: The future of cloud-native and modern application development: 25 years of perspective

10 years ago we heard that "software is eating the world" and now more we’re seeing containers do the same thing – rapidly changing established patterns of software development, while disrupting entire companies and industries. The container abstraction has made it possible to build, test and deploy applications that run consistently and reliably – and to move them between compute environments with better isolation and security. Yet this revolution is just getting started, with most technology spending still on-prem. Many business applications owners struggle to put advances like microservices, service mesh & CI/CD into practice; and securing the software “supply chain” is as complex as ever. Joshua Burgin was “in the room where it happened” over the last 25 years, and he will take you on a dive deep into the evolution of software and hardware abstractions, and offer some predictions about the coming decade – supported by real world examples from his days as one of the first 100 employees of Amazon.com, to his time at Zynga, AWS and now VMware Tanzu. Joshua will cover how pervasive containerization will transform not just how software gets written and deployed, but how databases, networking and security are done. He’ll also explore the breadth and depth of personal and professional opportunity that remains to transform the way enterprises, governments, and businesses of all sizes use technology to fulfill their missions.


BIO: Joshua Burgin is the VP of Product & Technical Strategy for the Tanzu Platform at VMWare. In this position he helps developers modernize their applications and embrace cloud-native patterns like microservices, containers, DevOps, and Kubernetes. Prior to joining VMWare in 2022, he spent 7.5 years at AWS in a variety of executive roles, most recently as the General Manager for AWS Outposts business, responsible for engineering, product management, design, marketing and business development. Before Outposts, Joshua worked as Chief of Staff & Technical Advisor to AWS’s most senior executives – Charlie Bell and Andy Jassy – tracking key industry trends, formulating strategy, and developing in-depth solutions to business & technical challenges. From 2014-2019, Joshua was a Director/General Manager in Compute Services – as well as the sponsor for 3 successful acquisitions (ClusterK, Thinkbox, Nimble Software). Prior to AWS, he held product and technology executive roles at several startups, including Zynga, where he drove the development of their in-game advertising platform.

SPOTLIGHT TALK: A Day in the Life of a Base Image: The Evolution of Vulnerabilities in the Most Popular Containers

Containers have become the nearly de facto way for developers to package and ship software today. On Docker Hub, which saw hundreds of billions container pulls in 2021 alone, there are 130 containers that constitute more than 25% of the annual pulls - seeing 31B+ pulls annually. These include the most popular DevOps and build tools, programming languages and databases, among others. While container scanning and security is becoming more widely adopted, it’s still not well-understood how these containers evolve over time from a security perspective. This includes understanding the long-term security posture of these containers, whether it is improving or declining as new vulnerabilities are discovered, as new versions are released and deployed to production. This talk will take a first-time look at why handling vulnerabilities in containers is a really sticky problem to begin with, with known vulnerabilities requiring patching, as new vulnerabilities arise constantly, and many other vulnerabilities simply falling into a catchall bucket of "won't fix" . We'll show data visualizations of how the attack surface of two mega-popular public DevOps and build container images have changed over the past year, highlighting the problem developers and DevSecOps teams are facing. We'll demonstrate how some of the most popular vulnerability scanners show different results, sometimes to extreme degrees. But stick around to the very end, because on the upside, we'll wrap up with practical steps developers can take to stay on top of vulnerabilities and prevent their dev process from grinding to a halt.
